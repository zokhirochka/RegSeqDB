Help

How to get the most out of RegSeqDB.

Overview

RegSeqDB provides two search interfaces for exploring promoter sequence variation, gene expression, and transcription factor binding data in E. coli. Both pages — Single Search and Compare Between Conditions — are accessible from the navigation bar at the top of every page. Results load dynamically below the form without refreshing the page.

Single Promoter Search — Inputs

The Single Promoter Search page allows you to retrieve expression and binding data for a specific promoter under a single experimental condition. You will need to provide three inputs: a Promoter Name, a Transcription Factor, and a Condition selected from the dropdown menu.

Promoter Name and Transcription Factor are case-sensitive and must match the database exactly — for example, tisBp or recAp for promoters, and LexA or CRP for transcription factors. The Condition dropdown contains all available experimental conditions, such as Glucose, Xylose, Heat Shock, and others.

Single Promoter Search — Results

Once you click Search, results will load below the form. Under Visualizations, you will see two scatter plots — TF Affinity vs. Expression and RNAP Binding Energy vs. Expression — both with OLS regression lines. Each plot has Zoom In, Zoom Out, and Reset controls.

If binding coordinate data is available, a Genomic Locus diagram will display the positions of the TSS, RNAP binding site, and TF binding site. Under Raw Results, a table shows sequence IDs, DNA and RNA barcode counts, expression ratios, TF affinity scores, and RNAP binding energies. If the table exceeds 50 rows, use Download CSV to export the full dataset.

Compare Between Conditions — Inputs

The Comparison page allows you to compare expression and binding data for the same promoter and transcription factor across two different experimental conditions. You will need to provide a Promoter Name, a Transcription Factor, a First Condition, and a Second Condition from the dropdown menus.

Compare Between Conditions — Results

Once you click Compare, results will load below the form. You will see two scatter plots — TF Affinity vs. Log Fold Change and RNAP Binding Energy vs. Log Fold Change — where Log Fold Change is computed as Log₂(Expression₁ / Expression₂). Both plots include OLS regression lines.

Input Format Notes

Promoter Name and Transcription Factor must match the database exactly, including capitalization. If your search returns no results, check for typos or try a different condition. Not all promoter and TF combinations have data under every experimental condition. For a quick test, try promoter tisBp with transcription factor LexA under Glucose.
